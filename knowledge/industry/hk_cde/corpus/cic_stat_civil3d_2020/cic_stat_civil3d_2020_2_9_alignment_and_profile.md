2.9 Alignment and Profile 
Alignments are a combination of horizontal lines, curves, and spirals that are viewed as 
one object. It can represent road centrelines, pipe networks, and other construction 
baselines. Creating and defining a horizontal alignment is one of the first steps in the 
roadway, railroad, or site design. Please refer to the links below for more information 
about alignments :

2.10 Corridor and Assembly 
A corridor can be used to model a variety of features, such as highways, channels, and 
runways. To model a corridor, users need first to define (i) Alignment and (ii) Profile and 
(iii) Assembly in Civil 3D. Besides, users may use the Civil 3D subassembly composer 
(SAC) to create parametric sections for the corridor.

2.11 Pipe Network, Part builder and Pressure Network 
Pipe network should be used in Civil 3D for underground utility systems, such as storm 
sewers or sanitary sewers.  A pipe network can contain pipes only, structures (such as 
manholes) only, or both pipes and structures. In this sample, surface channel is 
suggested to be modelled by corridor while catch pits are suggested to be modelled by 
structures in a pipe network.
